Xants

This poster features one of the fonts published from the Adobe Hidden Treasures project. There were two important subjects to focus on: the font itself and the original designer Xanti Schawinsky. Since the typeface takes precedence, the font was given a greater visual weight. Supporting text within this poster explains about Mr. Schawinsky and Adobe Hidden Treasures project.

This project was a team consisting of Tannor Glumbik, Anthony Riojas, Jose Arciga, and Payton White. Content provided by Mindy Breen, Professor, Eastern Washington University. Content sourced from Adobe Hidden Treasures.

Temperance

Temperance in its upright position is about balance; whilst in reverse, Temperance is about a lack of balance. Traditionally this card depicts an angelic figure, but for this redesign, the turkey vulture was chosen. While some view this bird of prey as evil or nasty, turkey vultures are creatures who clean and purify the environment.

Tannor Glumbik

Leadership. Beyond the fact that Tannor Glumbik is a creative and hard-working individual, what sets Tannor apart from other creative and hard-working people is leadership. He has displayed a strong affinity for helping others learn and leading individuals / groups toward goals. Tannor is currently inspired by his Bachelor of Design at Eastern Washington University – to be completed in Spring 2021 – and is further immersing himself in typography, sound design, graphic design in general, and pedagogical knowledge. Tannor’s career goal is to teach graphic design at a college institution.

Education

Eastern Washington University

Bachelor of Design & Business Administration Minor

Through EWU’s Bachelor of Design program, Tannor has learned the history of good design and “how to break the grid meaningfully”. This program has focused on Art Nouveau, Art Deco, Constructivism, the Bauhaus, and the Swiss International Style. Tannor is personally drawn to the simplistic, bold, geometric, and tension-filled works of the Bauhaus and Russian Constructivism. During this major, Tannor has focused on the Adobe Suite: Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Bridge, After Effects, etc. Of the many Adobe programs, Tannor is most learned and skilled in Illustrator and InDesign. Throughout Tannor’s learning and assignments, he has also incorporated sound design. Tannor has utilized MuseScore 3, Audacity, and Studio One 4. Other miscellaneous learning includes web design, UX design, and photography.

EWU’s Business Administration Minor prepares a student to be knowledgeable in various business facets and how to oversee these departments. Tannor has spent the majority of his minor studying management, accounting, and information systems.

Maestro Award

Seattle, WA 2016 & Vancouver, BC 2017

The World Strides Heritage Festivals are located around the world and are a gathering of bands to perform for ratings and critiques. A Maestro Award is only handed out to a select few, who display outstanding musicianship. Tannor was awarded the Maestro Award for his leading rolls in the MSHS Percussion Ensemble and MSHS Wind Ensemble. A marimba duet consisting of Tannor and Devin Petersen inspired the announcer to introduce the winning pair as the “Hot Marimba Players.”

Experience

The UPS Store Associate

Jan 2020 – Current

As an associate, Tannor has been trained to assist customers with their packing, shipping, and printing needs. In the area of print, Tannor has helped customers with a wide variety of printing needs. Most common are the day to day copies, but Tannor has also assisted with photo-restorations, calendar layouts, lamination, and other print projects.

Mt. Spokane High School Marching Band Percussion Instructor & Sound Engineer

Fall 2018 & 2019

For the Mt. Spokane High School Marching Band (MSMB), Tannor has been the Front Ensemble Tech for two seasons. This position includes teaching a group of students their show music and running the sound equipment. Using Tannor’s own musical experience of 15 years as an instrumental musician, he has taught students pitched percussion, auxiliary percussion, and keyboards. During the MSMB season, Tannor was responsible for a group of 12–16 students.

The front ensemble consists of instruments that are miked and electronic instruments. These microphones and electronic instruments are sent to a digital mixing console and output through a combination of speakers and subwoofers. Dependent on the show’s demands, Tannor was in charge of 15–30 channels. Tannor also teaches the front ensemble students how to set-up and tear-down the equipment for performances, where each band is given a time limit to set-up, play, and tear-down.
